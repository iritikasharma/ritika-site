Ritika
Sharma

Biochemist and systems thinker working at the frontier of sustainable nitrogen fixation and AI-driven automation — where molecular biology meets intelligent design.

My research at OU centers on engineering hybrid biocatalytic systems — specifically coupling CdS quantum dots with MoFe proteins derived from nitrogenase enzymes to drive light-powered dinitrogen reduction. The goal: a carbon-free route to ammonia synthesis that could reshape how the world feeds itself.

In parallel, I design and deploy AI automation pipelines and intelligent systems — translating the same first-principles thinking from the bench into scalable, adaptive architectures for the real world.
